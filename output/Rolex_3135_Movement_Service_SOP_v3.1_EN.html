Rolex 3135 Movement Complete Disassembly and Assembly - Standard Operating Procedure

3.0 - Detailed Version
2026年1月27日

Equipment

Rolex 3135 Automatic Mechanical Movement

Difficulty

Professional Grade (Requires Professional Watchmaking Training and Certification)

Estimated Time

90-120 minutes (Experienced Technician)

Required Tools

  • Precision Tweezers (2 pcs, 0.5mm tip width, anti-magnetic stainless steel)
  • Precision Screwdriver (flathead blade, 0.8mm width)
  • Torque Screwdriver (0.5 N·cm, optional)
  • Cleaning Cotton / Lint-free Cloth
  • Watch-specific Cleaning Solution
  • Air Blower (for dust removal)
  • Green Anti-slip Watch Repair Mat
  • Parts Sorting Tray
  • Case Back Opener
  • Spring Bar Tool (for bracelet installation)
  • Magnifier or Head-mounted Magnifier (5-10x)

Safety Notes

  • All operations must be performed in a dust-free environment with dust cover recommended
  • All tools must be cleaned and anti-magnetic treated
  • Ruby jewel bearings are extremely fragile and expensive - handle with extreme care
  • All disassembled parts must be recorded in sequence and photographed
  • Screw torque must be strictly controlled within 0.5 N·cm
  • No eating or smoking above the movement
  • Escapement mechanism (balance wheel) is very fragile - must be disassembled last and assembled first

Detailed Steps (36 steps)

1
Initial Movement State Inspection
Preparation Phase 00:00
Details:
Place Rolex 3135 movement flat on green repair mat, perform preliminary inspection using magnifier. Identify main components: gold main barrel (approx. 20mm diameter, left side), silver main plate (brushed finish), 4 purple-red ruby bearings (approx. 2mm diameter), gear train system, 8 screw holes (approx. 1.5mm diameter). Photograph initial state.

Required Tools

Green Watch Repair Mat, Magnifier, Camera

Warning

Ensure clean dust-free environment, check movement for obvious damage or abnormalities

2
Prepare to Remove Main Train Bridge
Disassembly Phase 00:39
Details:
Hold red precision tweezers (tip width 0.5mm) in right hand, approach train bridge area from upper right. Position tweezers tip 2mm from bridge, observe bridge fixing screw positions (2 flathead screws).

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers

Warning

Do not apply excessive force with tweezers - avoid scratching plate surface or touching ruby bearings

3
Extract Minute Pinion Gear
Disassembly Phase 01:01
Details:
Use precision tweezers to grip minute pinion gear (MINUTE PINION N°3135-335, OD 5mm, central ruby bearing, 15 teeth). Grip outer ring, lift vertically 3mm until gear separates. Place in compartment #1 of parts tray.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Parts Sorting Tray

Warning

Avoid touching central ruby bearing - maintain vertical lift to prevent jamming

4
Inspect Minute Pinion Gear Details
Disassembly Phase 01:03
Details:
Move gear to lens front (5cm distance) for close-up. Observe bearing hole (1mm diameter), ruby bearing setting, tooth surface polish quality. Record part number: MINUTE PINION (N°3135-335), recommended lubricant: MOEBIUS 9501.

Required Tools

Magnifier

Warning

Check ruby bearing for looseness or damage - replace if damaged

5
Prepare Two-Handed Main Train Bridge Removal
Disassembly Phase 01:16
Details:
Left hand holds 1st tweezers approaching left edge, right hand holds 2nd tweezers approaching right edge. Confirm positions of 3 ruby bearings (gold outer rings) and 2 fixing screw holes (flathead, 1.5mm diameter). Bridge thickness 1.5mm.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers (2 pcs)

Warning

Confirm bridge screws completely removed - forced lifting will damage screw holes

6
Two-Handed Coordinated Main Train Bridge Removal
Disassembly Phase 01:17
Details:
Left tweezers grip left edge (2mm width), right tweezers grip right edge. Apply even upward force simultaneously (50gf/side). Keep bridge horizontal. Initial lift 2mm, observe separation. Continue to 5mm until complete separation. Central ruby bearing (3mm diameter, gold ring) removes with bridge.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers (2 pcs)

Warning

Must coordinate both hands evenly - prevent single-side drop causing ruby bearing damage. Maintain horizontal - tilt angle not exceeding 5 degrees

7
Complete Train Bridge Removal and Inspect Movement
Disassembly Phase 01:18
Details:
Remove bridge completely (5mm+ height) and place in compartment #2. Inspect post-removal state: large gold barrel wheel (15mm diameter) exposed, auto rotor bridge (silver, ruby bearings) visible, 4 screw holes (3mm depth) undamaged. Record bridge number (e.g. 3135-130).

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Parts Sorting Tray

Warning

Immediately check bridge ruby bearings for looseness - inspect screw holes for thread damage

8
Flip Movement to Back for Automatic Mechanism Disassembly
Disassembly Phase 01:22
Details:
Carefully flip movement 180° with automatic mechanism facing up. Confirm visible: gold reversing wheel (center, 10mm diameter, radial pattern), silver rotor bridge (irregular shape, 3 screws), multiple gears (2-5mm diameter). Prepare screwdriver for bridge removal.

Required Tools

Precision Screwdriver (flathead, 0.8mm width)

Warning

Gentle flipping motion - avoid loose parts falling. Ensure stable placement

9
Remove 3 Fixing Screws from Automatic Rotor Bridge
Disassembly Phase 01:22-01:26
Details:
Use precision screwdriver (flathead, 0.8mm width) in diagonal sequence. Order: 1st (upper left) → 2nd (lower right) → 3rd (upper right). Each screw: 3-5 CCW turns to remove. Specs: flathead slot, 1.5mm diameter, 2mm length. Place in compartment #3, record quantity.

Required Tools

Precision Screwdriver, Parts Sorting Tray

Warning

Blade must fully embed in slot - avoid slipping causing slot damage. Moderate force only

10
Remove Automatic Rotor Bridge
Disassembly Phase 01:26
Details:
Use tweezers to grip bridge edge (left protruding part), lift vertically 3mm for complete separation. Specs: WINDING BRIDGE N°3135-130, irregular shape, '3135' engraving, 3 screw holes (1.5mm), 1 central bearing seat (4mm), 1.5mm thickness. Place in compartment #4.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Parts Sorting Tray

Warning

Maintain vertical lift - avoid tilting causing parts in bearing hole to fall

11
Inspect Automatic Rotor Bridge Details
Disassembly Phase 01:29
Details:
Place bridge flat on green mat for detailed inspection. Confirm: irregular curved edge intact, central bearing hole (for rotor shaft) unworn, left 2 and right 1 screw holes threads intact, surface brushed finish scratch-free. Photograph front state.

Required Tools

Magnifier, Camera

Warning

Check bearing hole inner wall for scratches/wear - record for lubrication attention during assembly

12
Classify and Display Disassembled Automatic System Parts
Disassembly Phase 01:40
Details:
Arrange rotor system parts on mat for photography. Layout: upper left - reversing wheel (gold gear, tweezers-held), center - rotor bridge body, lower left - 3 screws (flathead). Confirm all parts complete.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Camera

Warning

Confirm all small parts (especially screws/washers) properly placed to prevent loss

13
Inspect Crown Wheel Component
Disassembly Phase 01:41
Details:
Use tweezers to point at crown wheel (CROWN WHEEL N°3135-210) for close observation. Features: circular wheel with radial pattern (8mm diameter), approx. 20 teeth, central spring mechanism, installed in bridge groove. No disassembly - functional check only.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Magnifier

Warning

Crown wheel has internal spring - do not force disassemble to avoid spring ejection

14
Finger Press Test Crown Wheel Elasticity
Disassembly Phase 01:47
Details:
Use right index finger to press crown wheel center vertically, apply 100-200gf pressure, observe 0.5mm compression and rebound. Test purpose: confirm spring mechanism function normal, rebound force appropriate, no binding. Press 3-5 times for repeated testing.

Required Tools

Manual Operation

Warning

Moderate pressing force - avoid excessive force deforming spring. Confirm part returns to original position

15
Deep Disassembly of Entire Train System
Disassembly Phase 01:51-03:34
Details:
Continue disassembly in sequence: (1) Remove 2 escapement bridge screws → (2) Remove escapement bridge (with ruby bearings) → (3) Extract balance wheel assembly (most delicate, 10mm diameter, extra care) → (4) Extract pallet fork (6mm length) → (5) Remove escape wheel (8mm diameter, 15 teeth) → (6) Sequentially disassemble gear train: second wheel, third wheel, fourth wheel (2-8mm diameter). Place each part in corresponding tray compartment, photograph sequence.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Precision Screwdriver, Parts Sorting Tray, Camera

Warning

Balance wheel most fragile - must grip balance staff ends only, never touch wheel body. Record all gear sequence, recommend numbering labels

16
Movement Back Deep Cleaning
Cleaning & Maintenance Phase 03:56
Details:
Comprehensive cleaning of movement back: (1) Use tweezers with cleaning cotton dipped in watch cleaning solution, gently wipe rotor mounting bearing seat → (2) Use air blower to remove metal debris from screw holes → (3) Wipe ruby bearing areas with cleaning cotton, remove oil/dust → (4) Inspect movement baseplate for scratches/corrosion. After cleaning, only main barrel and base frame remain.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Cleaning Cotton, Watch Cleaning Solution, Air Blower

Warning

Do not use excessive cleaning solution - avoid penetration into bearing holes. Use lint-free cotton to avoid residue. Gentle wiping only

17
Clean and Inspect All Disassembled Parts
Cleaning & Maintenance Phase 04:00-08:30
Details:
Clean and inspect each disassembled part: (1) Soak small parts (gears, bearings) in cleaning solution 3-5 minutes → (2) Gently brush gear teeth with soft brush → (3) Dry all parts with cleaning cotton → (4) Inspect each part with magnifier: gear tooth wear, ruby bearing looseness, screw thread damage, bridge surface cracks → (5) Rearrange cleaned parts in assembly order. Record parts needing replacement (if any).

Required Tools

Watch Cleaning Solution, Soft Brush, Cleaning Cotton, Magnifier, Camera

Warning

Balance wheel assembly cannot be soaked - only gently wipe with cotton swab. Loose ruby bearings must be replaced, cannot continue use

18
Begin Assembly - Install Escape Wheel System (Key Turning Point)
Assembly Phase 08:39
Details:
Flip movement to front (opposite from disassembly) to begin assembly. First step - install escape wheel system: (1) Apply lubricant to escape wheel bearing hole (MOEBIUS 9010, 0.01mm³) → (2) Use tweezers to grip escape wheel (8mm diameter) staff end, align to bearing hole → (3) Insert vertically downward, 2mm depth → (4) Gently rotate escape wheel 360°, confirm smooth rotation without binding.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Lubricant MOEBIUS 9010, Oil Pen

Warning

Minimal lubricant quantity - excess causes oil spread. Must maintain vertical insertion - tilting damages bearing hole

19
Install Pallet Fork
Assembly Phase 08:45
Details:
Install pallet fork (6mm length): (1) Apply lubricant to pallet fork bearing hole (MOEBIUS 9010) → (2) Grip pallet fork center with tweezers, align to bearing hole → (3) Insert vertically, ensure fork horns correctly engage escape wheel teeth → (4) Gently push pallet, observe if escape wheel rotates accordingly, confirm proper meshing.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Lubricant

Warning

Pallet fork is core component - must ensure fork horn to escape wheel tooth clearance correct (0.02mm), otherwise affects timekeeping accuracy

20
Install Balance Wheel Assembly
Assembly Phase 08:50
Details:
Install most delicate balance wheel assembly (10mm diameter): (1) Apply minimal lubricant to balance bearing hole (MOEBIUS 9010, 0.005mm³) → (2) Two-handed operation: left tweezers grip balance staff upper end, right tweezers support balance lower end → (3) Align balance staff vertically to bearing hole, slowly insert → (4) After releasing tweezers, gently blow balance wheel to free swing, observe amplitude should be ≥270°, swing duration ≥30 seconds.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers (2 pcs), Lubricant

Warning

Balance wheel most fragile component - never touch wheel body, only contact staff ends. Do not force rotation after insertion - let it freely swing for testing

21
Install and Secure Escapement Bridge
Assembly Phase 09:00
Details:
Install escapement bridge (with 2 ruby bearings): (1) Confirm balance and escape wheel positions correct → (2) Grip bridge with tweezers, place horizontally on movement, align 2 screw holes → (3) Tighten 2 fixing screws with screwdriver in diagonal sequence (1st→2nd) → (4) Torque control: approx. 0.5 N·cm each, use torque driver or hand feel → (5) Gently blow balance again, confirm amplitude and duration unaffected.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Precision Screwdriver, Torque Screwdriver (optional)

Warning

Do not over-tighten screws - excessive torque deforms bridge pressing balance staff, affecting timekeeping. Must retest balance swing after tightening

22
Sequentially Install Gear Train
Assembly Phase 09:02-09:35
Details:
Install gear train in sequence: (1) Third wheel (5mm diameter): apply lubricant → align bearing hole → insert vertically → test rotation → (2) Fourth wheel (4mm diameter): same steps, confirm meshing with third wheel → (3) Second wheel (6mm diameter): same steps → (4) Minute pinion (5mm diameter, first gear removed): align installation position, ensure proper meshing with hour wheel. After installing each gear, must gently rotate 360°, confirm smooth rotation without binding, proper meshing with adjacent gears, no abnormal noise.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Lubricant, Oil Pen

Warning

Gear installation sequence cannot be reversed - must strictly follow disassembly reverse order. Each gear meshing clearance 0.02-0.05mm - too tight or loose affects timekeeping

23
Install Main Train Bridge
Assembly Phase 09:41
Details:
Reinstall initially removed main train bridge: (1) Confirm 3 ruby bearings on bridge intact and clean → (2) Grip bridge edges with both tweezers, align horizontally to mounting position → (3) Slowly lower, ensure 3 gear staffs below correctly insert into bridge bearing holes → (4) Gently press bridge flush to movement baseplate → (5) Tighten 2 fixing screws in diagonal sequence (torque 0.5 N·cm) → (6) Rotate barrel wheel, confirm entire train operates smoothly.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers (2 pcs), Precision Screwdriver

Warning

When placing bridge, must ensure all gear staffs align to bearing holes simultaneously - single-side entry first bends staff ends. Must test train after tightening screws

24
Install Remaining Bridges and Train Components
Assembly Phase 10:00-12:00
Details:
Continue installing remaining components on movement front: (1) Install calendar mechanism (if applicable) → (2) Install hour wheel and minute wheel → (3) Install related bridges and secure → (4) Check all fixing screws tightened → (5) Manually rotate movement, test complete train function: rotate barrel → observe all gears rotating → confirm escapement mechanism working properly → observe second wheel rotation speed (should be 1/5 revolution per second, i.e. 6°/second).

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Precision Screwdriver, Lubricant

Warning

All bridge screws must be tightened in diagonal sequence to prevent bridge deformation. If any abnormality during function test (binding, noise, rough rotation), must immediately stop and recheck

25
Complete Train System Inspection
Assembly Phase 12:31
Details:
Comprehensive inspection of assembled train system: (1) Visual check: large gold gear train (triple gear set, 12mm diameter) positioned correctly, barrel area (gold, 15mm diameter) not loose, escapement regulator (balance 10mm diameter) swinging normally, all bridges (silver, ruby bearings) flush fit, approx. 6 fixing screws all tight → (2) Function test: manually wind 10 turns → observe second wheel rotation → measure balance amplitude (should be ≥270°) → listen to movement operation sound (should be even and regular, no abnormal noise). Photograph assembled state.

Required Tools

Camera, Amplitude Meter (optional), Winding Tool

Warning

If amplitude below 270°, indicates excessive train resistance or insufficient lubrication - need recheck. If abnormal noise present, must disassemble to locate noise source

26
Install Dial-Side Hour/Minute Hand Mechanism
Assembly Phase 13:23-13:46
Details:
Install components connecting to dial on movement front: (1) Install minute wheel (7mm diameter): align to central shaft hole, ensure meshing with minute pinion → (2) Install hour wheel (9mm diameter): fit around minute wheel outer ring, ensure coaxiality → (3) Install hand-setting mechanism: including clutch lever, setting wheel etc. → (4) Test hand-setting function: pull out crown → rotate crown → observe if hour/minute hands follow rotation → hour hand should rotate at 1/12 speed of minute hand → push in crown, confirm clutch reset.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Lubricant

Warning

Hour/minute wheels must ensure coaxiality, deviation cannot exceed 0.05mm, otherwise causes hand jamming. Do not use excessive force during hand-setting test - avoid damaging setting mechanism

27
Flip Movement to Back to Begin Automatic Winding Assembly
Assembly Phase 14:01
Details:
Carefully flip movement 180° to automatic mechanism side up. Confirm preparation: (1) Auto rotor reversing wheel and related gears cleaned and lubricated → (2) Auto rotor bridge clean and intact → (3) 3 fixing screws ready → (4) Confirm front train system not loosened during flipping.

Warning

Gentle flipping motion - avoid impact to installed components. Ensure stable placement after flipping

28
Install Automatic Rotor Reversing Wheel
Assembly Phase 14:33
Details:
Install auto rotor reversing wheel (gold, 10mm diameter, radial pattern): (1) Apply lubricant to reversing wheel bearing seat (MOEBIUS 9010) → (2) Grip reversing wheel center shaft with tweezers, align to bearing seat → (3) Insert vertically, ensure wheel's lower gear properly meshes with internal train wheel → (4) Gently rotate reversing wheel 360°, confirm smooth rotation without binding, proper gear meshing, no abnormal noise or resistance.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Lubricant

Warning

Reversing wheel must ensure complete meshing with lower train gear, clearance 0.02-0.05mm. Rotation test should feel even resistance, no binding points

29
Install and Secure Automatic Rotor Bridge
Assembly Phase 14:55
Details:
Reinstall auto rotor bridge (WINDING BRIDGE N°3135-130): (1) Confirm bridge orientation correct ('3135' engraving up) → (2) Grip bridge edge with tweezers, align horizontally to mounting position → (3) Slowly lower, ensure central bearing hole correctly fits on reversing wheel shaft → (4) Align 3 screw holes → (5) Tighten 3 fixing screws in diagonal sequence: 1st (upper left, 0.5 N·cm) → 2nd (lower right, 0.5 N·cm) → 3rd (upper right, 0.5 N·cm) → (6) Confirm bridge flush fit, no warping or tilting.

Required Tools

Precision Tweezers, Precision Screwdriver, Torque Screwdriver (optional)

Warning

Screws must be tightened in diagonal sequence to prevent bridge deformation. After tightening, gently rotate reversing wheel to confirm rotation unaffected

30
Test Automatic Winding Mechanism Function
Assembly Phase 15:00
Details:
Test if auto winding mechanism working properly: (1) Hold movement, simulate wrist motion (shake left-right, amplitude 30°, frequency 2x/second) → (2) Observe if reversing wheel follows motion and rotates → (3) Rotation should be smooth and bidirectional (clockwise and counterclockwise) → (4) Listen to auto winding sound (should be light clicking, regular rhythm) → (5) After stopping motion, reversing wheel should stop quickly, no inertial coasting (coasting not exceeding 1/4 turn).

Warning

If reversing wheel does not rotate or rotation not smooth, check gear meshing and lubrication. If abnormal noise or excessive inertial coasting, need recheck bridge installation

31
Install Movement into Watch Case
Final Assembly Phase 15:13
Details:
Install assembled movement into Rolex case (stainless steel, 40mm diameter): (1) Confirm case interior clean without dust → (2) Check movement holder intact → (3) Left hand holds case (crown position toward 3 o'clock), right hand holds movement → (4) Confirm movement orientation correct (crown stem aligned to case crown hole) → (5) Slowly insert movement into case, ensure even descent on all sides → (6) Gently press movement edge (pressure 200gf), seat movement completely → (7) Install movement holder, rotate clockwise to lock (approx. 90° rotation).

Required Tools

Movement Holder Tool (optional)

Warning

Ensure correct movement orientation during insertion - misalignment prevents crown installation. Even pressure when pressing - do not apply single-side force. Holder must be locked tight, otherwise movement loosens

32
Install Transparent Case Back
Final Assembly Phase 15:29
Details:
Install transparent case back (sapphire crystal): (1) Check case back seal (O-ring) intact, no aging cracks → (2) Apply thin layer of silicone grease on seal (improves water resistance) → (3) Correctly install seal in case back groove → (4) Align case back to case rear, ensure correct orientation (threads aligned) → (5) Use case back opener or manually rotate case back, clockwise rotation approx. 180° until resistance significantly increases (indicates locked) → (6) Check if case back flush with case, even gap.

Required Tools

Case Back Opener, Silicone Grease

Warning

Seal must be intact - replace if damaged, otherwise loses water resistance. Do not use excessive force when tightening - avoid damaging threads. Check if case back level after tightening

33
Install Bracelet
Final Assembly Phase 16:18
Details:
Connect metal bracelet (Rolex Oyster) to case: (1) Check case lugs (connection holes on case sides) intact → (2) Prepare 2 spring bars (1.5mm diameter, 20mm length) → (3) Use spring bar tool to compress spring bar end → (4) Insert spring bar ends into lug hole and bracelet hole respectively → (5) Release spring bar tool, confirm spring bar fully engaged in lug hole → (6) Repeat steps to install second spring bar on other case side → (7) Pull test bracelet (pull force approx. 2kg), confirm secure connection.

Required Tools

Spring Bar Tool, Spring Bar (2 pcs)

Warning

Pay attention to orientation when installing spring bar, ensure spring end faces outward. Must pull test after installation to prevent detachment during wear

34
Comprehensive Function Testing
Final Assembly Phase 16:39
Details:
Perform function tests on complete watch: (1) Winding test: rotate crown clockwise 20-30 turns, feel resistance gradually increasing (indicates mainspring wound) → (2) Hand-setting test: pull crown to position 1 (quick-set calendar, if applicable) or position 2 (hand-setting) → rotate crown → observe if hour/minute hands rotate smoothly → hour hand should rotate at 1/12 speed of minute hand → push crown to original position → (3) Timekeeping test: observe second hand operation → second hand should rotate smoothly, beat 5x per second (28800 bph movement) → no binding or backward movement → (4) Auto winding test: shake watch left-right (amplitude 30°) → listen for auto rotor rotation sound.

Warning

Do not use excessive force when winding - stop when resistance significantly increases to avoid over-tensioning mainspring. Gentle motion during hand-setting - do not rapidly rotate crown

35
Appearance Cleaning and Final Inspection
Quality Inspection Phase 17:41
Details:
Perform final cleaning and inspection of watch: (1) Wipe case surface with lint-free cloth, remove fingerprints and oil → (2) Wipe watch crystal and transparent case back with specialized glass cleaner → (3) Clean each bracelet link, use soft brush to remove dust from gaps → (4) Inspect all case areas: lugs for scratches, crown for looseness, case back for secure lock, bracelet connection for firmness → (5) Check transparent case back: confirm movement visible, no fingerprints/fogging, seal positioned correctly → (6) Final appearance check: no obvious scratches, no dust, no oil, overall good luster.

Required Tools

Lint-free Cleaning Cloth, Glass Cleaning Solution, Soft Brush

Warning

Avoid cleaning solution entering case interior during cleaning. Use specialized cleaner when wiping crystal to avoid water marks

36
Timekeeping Accuracy Test and Quality Confirmation
Quality Inspection Phase 17:48
Details:
Perform final timekeeping accuracy test and quality confirmation: (1) Place watch on timegrapher for timekeeping test (or use mobile app for simple test) → (2) Record timekeeping data: instantaneous daily rate (should be within ±2 seconds/day), amplitude (should be ≥270°), beat error (should be <1ms) → (3) Listen to movement operation sound: should be regular ticking, even frequency, no abnormal noise → (4) Function confirmation checklist: ✓ Auto winding function normal ✓ Hand-setting function normal ✓ Manual winding function normal ✓ Second hand operation smooth ✓ No abnormal noise ✓ Appearance intact ✓ Water seal intact → (5) Record completion time and technician signature → (6) Notify customer watch service completed.

Required Tools

Timegrapher (optional), Mobile Timegrapher App (optional)

Warning

If timekeeping accuracy not within standard range (±2 seconds/day), need recheck movement train system. 24-hour timekeeping test required after service completion to ensure long-term stability

Quality Check Standards

Timekeeping Accuracy Test: Instantaneous daily rate within ±2 seconds/day
Amplitude Test: Balance wheel amplitude ≥270 degrees
Function Test: Automatic winding, manual winding, hand-setting all functions normal
Appearance Inspection: No scratches, no dust, seals intact
Sound Detection: Movement operation sound regular and even, no abnormal noise
Water Resistance: Case back seal intact, water resistance function normal
24-hour Timekeeping Test: Recommend 24-hour continuous timekeeping test after service completion

Important Notes

This SOP applies to complete disassembly and assembly of Rolex 3135 automatic mechanical movement
Operation requires professional tools and clean dust-free environment
Recommended to perform under guidance of experienced watchmaker - beginners not recommended for independent operation
All parts must be assembled in reverse order of disassembly - recommend photographing each step
Lubricant usage should be minimal - excessive amount causes oil spread affecting timekeeping
Ruby jewel bearings are expensive and fragile components - damage requires replacement with genuine parts
Screw torque controlled at 0.5 N·cm - avoid stripping or breaking
Must perform 24-hour timekeeping test after service completion to ensure accuracy meets standards
If any parts need replacement, must use Rolex genuine parts
Service records should be properly preserved including disassembly photos, parts list, timekeeping data, etc.
